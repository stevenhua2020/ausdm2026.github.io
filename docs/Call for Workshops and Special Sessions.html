The 24th Australasian Data Science and Machine Learning Conference (AusDM'26)

Sydney, Australia | 02-04 December 2026

Call for Workshops and Special Sessions

We invite proposals for workshops and special sessions to be held as part of the AUSDM’26 program. The purpose of workshops and special sessions is to bring participants with shared interests together to discuss a focused topic relevant to conference themes to exchange ideas and share latest research findings and technical outcomes.

While the format of a special session can be similar to a regular session of the technical program, a workshop can be organized in a more interactive and informal manner like a mini-conference and could last up to a half day. The organizers can also choose to hold their workshop or special session in either an in-person or hybrid mode.

Workshops can accept abstracts, research papers and position papers. Depending on the choice of the organizers, all peer-reviewed submissions of workshops and special sessions can be included in the AusDM'26 proceedings.

If the workshop research papers are to be included in the conference proceedings, all research papers should be between 6 and 15 pages in Springer CCIS style, as detailed on conference website and be peer-reviewed.

Submission Instructions

Proposal submissions should be directed to the Workshop Chairs using the subject “AUSDM 2026 workshop and special session proposal”. Please provide a Word or PDF document containing:

  • Type of the session: workshop or special topic session
  • Title
  • Presentation mode: in person or hybrid
  • Brief description of topics to be discussed or addressed: explain the goal of the proposed session, how the papers will be solicited, and why it is of interest to conference participants
  • Expected number of participants and submissions
  • A list of potential paper titles and contributing authors with affiliations (optional)
  • Organisers (minimum of two): names, affiliations, and short bio
  • If submissions are to be included in the conference proceedings: list potential program committee members

We expect that organizers are proactive in the field and that at least one organizer will attend the workshop or special session they are organizing. The accepted workshops and special topic sessions will be listed on the conference website. Note that it is up to the organizers to solicit and handle the paper submissions and reviews. The review system and final acceptance decisions should be managed in coordination with the Workshops and Special Session Chairs.
