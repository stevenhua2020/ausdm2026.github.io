The 24th Australasian Data Science and Machine Learning Conference (AusDM'26)

Sydney, Australia | 02-04 December 2026

Call for Doctoral Consortium

The doctoral consortium (DC) of AusDM'26 aims to provide Early Career Researchers (ECRs) and PhD students with the opportunity to meet, receive feedback on their research from established experts, and interact closely with their peers. Beyond valuable feedback, the consortium offers participants new contacts and great networking opportunities.

We do not expect you to have addressed all research questions, nor expect you to have completed the PhD. If you are a PhD candidate, you are expected to be at least 1.5 years into your PhD and have developed a clear topic and research approach and have made some progress. This consortium is ideal for discussing your research questions with experienced researchers and fellow students to receive informed feedback.

PhDs and ECRs with an accepted paper at the main AusDM'26 conference are also encouraged to submit and attend the DC session.

Submission and Participation

Submissions should be directed to the DC Chairs using the subject “AUSDM 2026 doctoral consortium submission”, and will be peer-reviewed and selected by the DC chairs and DC mentors. The final version of accepted Doctoral Consortium submissions will be included in the conference proceedings published by Springer. DC participants are expected to register and attend in person the Doctoral Consortium workshop as part of the conference program.

Submission should be a single PDF of two parts:

  1. This part should be between 6–8 pages in Springer CCIS style, as detailed on the conference website. It should include the following content (section titles may vary according to standard research paper practice):
    • Title of PhD proposal
    • Name of first author (must be a PhD student)
    • Name(s) of other co-author(s) (could be ECR(s) and/or supervisor(s))
    • The problem definitions, motivations, and novelties
    • Research questions and related work that frames your research
    • Your proposed approach and methodology, and its significance
    • The current results and future work of your research
    • Expected study contributions to research and practice
  2. A letter of nomination from your primary PhD supervisor, indicating support for your participation in the DC and your 1–2 page CV. This part will not be published.
