Grazing The Future - Putting the AI in Agri

Dr. Kenny Sabir

AgriWebb

LinkedIn

Abstract:

Machine learning is transforming how agricultural decisions are made — not just in research labs, but on-farm. The Foragecaster program, a $7.5M, four-year Food Agility CRC collaboration led by AgriWebb with research partners including UTS, QUT, UNE Smart Farm, and Cibo Labs demonstrates how predictive models can be integrated into farm management systems to deliver value directly to producers. This talk will share the design and implementation of Foragecaster’s machine learning models across three domains: Livestock growth forecasting (leveraging over 60 million animal records from AgriWebb’s database), Pasture growth and quality forecasting (combining biophysical models, climate forecasts, and remote sensing), Sustainability analysis (including carbon sequestration, emissions efficiency, ground cover and water metrics). We will show how these predictive layers are integrated into new planning tools, such as a grazing planner, enabling producers to plan stock movements, feed budgets, and sustainability outcomes over a six-month horizon. The talk will also highlight the challenges of integrating heterogeneous data sources, the importance of explainability in agricultural ML, and lessons from working with producers through design workshops, beta farms, and deep-dive case studies. Foragecaster illustrates how large-scale agricultural datasets, machine learning, and farmer co-design can be combined to move beyond prototypes and deliver trusted, operational AI that builds resilience and sustainability in livestock systems.

Short Biography:

Dr. Kenneth Sabir, VP of R&D at AgriWebb, was instrumental as the company's first employee and former Head of Engineering, helping it grow to support 20,000 livestock producers globally. He now spearheads Foragecaster, a four-year research project with the Food Agility CRC, QUT, UTS, and industry partners, focused on developing AI-powered planning tools for improved agricultural productivity and resilience. This initiative leverages Kenneth's extensive background in delivering leading-edge software for companies like IBM and Bell Labs, where he held technical leadership roles in worldwide teams as well as researched in fields like bio-inspired computing and stream processing. His diverse prior experience also includes medical research as the Bioinformatics Architect at the Garvan Institute and has also developed software for water management with the Irrigation Futures CRC. Kenneth holds 14 patents and has authored a dozen peer-reviewed research papers across fields such as database design, data visualisation techniques, stream processing, text-mining, pasture growth ML, remote sensing, bio-inspired computing.
