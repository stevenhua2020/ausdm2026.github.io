The 24th Australasian Data Science and Machine Learning Conference (AusDM'26)

Sydney, Australia | 02-04 December 2026

Call For Tutorials

We invite proposals for tutorials to be held as part of the AusDM'26 program. We will consider all topics, so long as the proposal makes a convincing argument that the tutorial is important and will be beneficial to the AusDM community. Tutorials should represent a sufficiently mature area of research or practice, covering both foundational advances and emerging directions.

Tutorial Format

  • Tutorials will be in-person and can be 2 hours long, including time for questions.
  • Each tutorial may have up to two presenters. Each presenter will be offered a complementary full conference registration. 
  • Tutorials are expected to be accessible to PhD candidates and the conference participants. Sharing of the presentation slides and codes are encouraged.

Submission Instructions

Proposal submissions should be directed to the Tutorial Chairs using the subject “AUSDM 2026 tutorial proposal”. Tutorial proposals should clearly describe:

  • The tutorial’s scope and learning objectives.
  • The specific content to be covered. 
  • The intended audience and prerequisites.
  • The presenter(s) and their affiliation(s).
  • Any planned supplementary materials (e.g., slides, notes, code).

Review Process

All proposals will be reviewed by the AusDM Tutorial Chairs. Selection will be based on relevance to the specified topics, clarity of presentation, expected impact on the community, and balance across topics and presenters.
